adding a unique identifier randomly to nicknames¶

In order to be able to test the quality of my results,
I'm going to add a suffix to usernames in random (p=0.5).
The suffix would be "#" at the end of the nickname.
In other words, if someones username is "John",
in about a half of the articles his name will be "John"
and in about another half of the articles his name will be "John#".

checking the rank of each "created" nick against the original nick¶

As explained before, I took each nick, and about half of the time I left the nick as is, and about another half of the times I appended "#" to the nick.
What I am checking now is, in the similarity measure table I created (that uses cosine-similarity),
what will be the average rank of each "created" nick to the original nick.
For sure, the similarity of a nick to itself will be the highest, the rank will be 0, and the similarity will always be 1.
But as we know that the original nick and the created nick (the #-appended nick) are basically the same person,
if the method works well the average rank of the #-appended nick should be low. As there are 3514 nicks (1757 original nicks and 1757 "fake" nicks that corresponds to the original nicks) - a totally random method will yield an average rank of about 1757 (the average case). As close as the average rank would be to 0 - as good as we will know the method is.

def tfidf_from_text(text):
    tf=Counter(text.split(' ')).most_common()
    tfidf={get_display(x[0]):x[1]*word2idf.get(x[0],0) for x in tf if word2idf.get(x[0],0)>0}
    return tfidf
